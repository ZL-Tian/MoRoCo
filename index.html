Abstract

Fleets of autonomous robots are often deployed along with several human operators to explore unknown scenes, identify potential features and take actions in various scenarios, e.g., subterranean exploration, reconnaissance, search and rescue missions. In such scenarios, communication among the robots and operators can be severely restricted to only close-range exchange via ad-hoc networks. Some recent works consider collaborative exploration under limited communication, which however mostly neglect the crucial rule of human operators, and the online interactions between the operators and the robotic fleets. Particularly, the operators may: (I) require timely feedback regarding the exploration progress and fleet status (II) prioritize or cancel certain regions or move dynamically; and (III) request real-time access to the video streams or control commands of certain robots. On the other hand, the robots may: (I) request human confirmation of unusual events or certain actions; and (II) ask for assistance to recover from planning or motion failures. To facilitate these interactions under scarce communication, a complete framework called MoRoCo is proposed in this work for the online coordination, interaction and exploration of multi-operator multi-robot systems. Depending on the type of requests, the robotic teams and operators switch smoothly and optimally between the “spread mode” for simultaneous fast exploration and intermittent exchange of sensory data and coordination messages; the “migrate mode” for swift migration to a different region as a team; and the “chain mode” to form multi-hop networks for a reliable high-bandwidth transfer while maintaining connectivity in motion. It is ensured that these bilateral and multi modal requests can be fulfilled online via only local communications and distributed coordination. Extensive large scale human-in-the-loop simulations and hardware experiments are performed over numerous challenging scenes and diverse online interactions, which validates not only the necessity of human-fleet interaction but also the efficiency of the proposed framework under scarce communication.

Overall Framework

Illustration of the proposed framework MoRoCo, for the coordination, supervision and interaction of multi-operator multi-robot systems that operate in unknown environments under scarce communication. Bilateral and multi-modal interactions during exploration are explicitly modeled including 7 types of operator-robot and operator-operator requests. Three operation modes as “spread, migrate and chain” modes are designed to facilitate the distributed communication and the fulfillment of these requests.
